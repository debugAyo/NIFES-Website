About NIFES Gidan Kwano
A campus fellowship with a clear mission

NIFES Gidan Kwano is the FUT Minna expression of the Nigerian Fellowship of Evangelical Students. We are a Christ-centered student movement formed to nurture faith, build godly character, and raise leaders who serve God with purpose.

We value Scripture, prayer, fellowship, holiness, service, and leadership shaped by Christ.

Who We Are

NIFES at a Glance

The Nigerian Fellowship of Evangelical Students (NIFES) is an indigenous student-centered and Christ-centered movement committed to raising godly leaders in higher institutions.

At FUT Minna, NIFES Gidan Kwano serves as a home for students who want to grow spiritually, belong deeply, and live missionally. Our gatherings are built to help members know God, serve one another, and reach the campus with the gospel.

We believe that a student's years on campus are pivotal. It's during these formative years that faith is solidified, leadership skills are honed, and kingdom priorities are established. Every programme, every Bible study, every outreach is designed with this vision in mind.

Whether you're seeking deeper spirituality, authentic community, or a platform to make a difference on campus, NIFES is the place for you.

Our Cardinal Strategies
01
Word-Centered Teaching
We equip students to encounter Jesus through Scripture. Our Bible studies, devotionals, and teaching emphasize the centrality of God's Word in every aspect of student life and decision-making.
02
Intentional Discipleship
Growth happens in relationships. We foster one-on-one mentoring, small group discussions, and accountability partnerships that help members grow deeper in faith and character.
03
Campus & Community Engagement
We're committed to being a redemptive presence on campus and in surrounding communities. Through outreach, service, and proclamation, we demonstrate Christ's love in word and deed.
04
Leadership Formation
We identify, nurture, and develop student leaders who will shape the future of Nigeria. Leadership training, spiritual mentoring, and strategic involvement prepare them for kingdom influence.
